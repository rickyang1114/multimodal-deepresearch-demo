Policy Landscape: Key Programs Supporting Senior Housing Development and Operations
Program/Initiative Housing Impact Service Integration Key Considerations
Federal Programs
HUD Section 232/223 Programs
Non-recourse permanent debt with rate-sensitive pricing Limited service integration Execution times: ~60 days for 223(a)(7) refis; ~6 months for 223(f)/232; 9-12 months for Section 232 expansion
Low Income Housing Tax Credits (LIHTC)
Critical equity source for affordable senior housing Varies by QAP requirements Example: $20M LIHTC equity for 990 Polk project ($309K/unit)
Medicaid 1115 Demonstrations
Limited direct housing funding Enables Tenancy Sustaining Services (TSS) CMS-approved demos allow braiding of Medicaid/non-Medicaid funding
Medicaid 1915(i) State Plan Options
Can fund home modifications Covers pre-tenancy/tenancy supports Requires standardized service definitions and PMPM rates
Section 8 Housing Choice Vouchers
Rental subsidy for low-income seniors No direct service funding Mainstream Vouchers can be targeted to seniors with disabilities
State Programs
LIHTC Qualified Allocation Plans (QAPs)
Sets priorities for tax credit allocation Can require service integration Example: Maryland DHCD's requirement for Permanent Supportive Housing plus TSS
Medicaid Redesign Initiatives
Supportive housing capital funding Service funding via various Medicaid authorities Example: NY MRT allocated $127M annually (FY 2015-17) with $47M for capital
Local Initiatives
Municipal Housing Trust Funds
Gap financing for affordable development Generally separate from services Example: $14.6M from SF Mayor's Office of Housing for 990 Polk
