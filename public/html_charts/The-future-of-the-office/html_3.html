The Evolution from Visual-Focused to Multi-Sensory Workplace Design
Comparing traditional 'Instagram-friendly' design with immersive multi-sensory environments for 2025
Traditional Approach
Multi-Sensory Experience
Sight
Static, visually appealing backgrounds optimized for photos and brand imagery
Dynamic lighting that shifts throughout day to match circadian rhythms; zoned visual experiences for different work modes
Sound
Basic acoustic treatments to reduce noise; generic background music in common areas
Zoned soundscapes tailored to activities; nature sounds in relaxation areas; focused white noise in concentration zones
Touch
Standard ergonomic furniture; consistent materials throughout spaces
Varied textures and materials that signal transitions between zones; temperature gradients for different activities; tactile feedback elements
Smell
Air fresheners or scent marketing limited to reception areas
Subtle aromatherapy elements to enhance focus, creativity, or relaxation; integration with ventilation systems for freshness
Source: Adapted from Gensler "Transformation Economy" framework (2025)
