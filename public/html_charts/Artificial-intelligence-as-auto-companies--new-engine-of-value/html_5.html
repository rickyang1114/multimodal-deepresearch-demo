AI-Powered Quality Control: Audi Neckarsulm Case Study
Comparison of AI-based and traditional quality inspection methods
AI-Based System (WPS-Analytics) Traditional Manual Inspection
Welds inspected per shift 1.5 million spot welds (300 vehicles) ~5,000 welds per vehicle (sampling)
Coverage 100% inline weld coverage Limited sampling (~5-10%)
Detection capability Real-time detection of all defect types Limited to major defect types
Labor requirement 30-50% reduction in QC labor costs Baseline (high labor intensity)
Additional benefits Predictive maintenance, process optimization Limited to defect detection
Implementation Architecture:
  • Intel Xeon edge nodes for data ingestion
  • Processes data from welding-gun controllers
  • Captures voltage/current curves, weld configuration
  • Monitors metal type and electrode health
  • Real-time analysis with YOLOv3-based spot-weld detection
Infrastructure rollout underway at Audi Brussels, VW Emden, and Ingolstadt
Sources: Itransition, Audi AG data (as of 06/30/2023), and Wei Dai et al. (Feb 2021)
